The braid

The Weave

On paper, we inscribed our care—and the felt-sense it potentiates. That act carried something from the actual back into the virtual. This weave let our shared desire-as-care, rather than desire-as-lack, signal toward resonant language in the readings gathered at this portal’s opening. We are, and have always been, in conversation with everyone who has ever been—their externalized memories permeating the shared field of perception. The quality of attention we bring to the dialogue affords us deeper and deeper experience.

With apologies to anyone who was present and whose lines somehow did not make it into the weave. Like digital compression eroding the quality of a file, the lived haptic textures of being-with others can analogously yield qualitative losses—even as we are wholly absorbed in the gains of interpersonal presence.

I The self, the body, presence
1

Relationship to my body’s knowing—it feels boring and hard!

The boring is not the obstacle to the knowing. It may be where the knowing hatches.

If sleep represents the high point of bodily relaxation, deep boredom is the peak of mental relaxation. A purely hectic rush produces nothing new.

Byung-Chul Han, The Burnout Society
2

Myself—how to be present when I’m in flow; tinkering, without judgement or expectation. Sometimes disappointed by little progress.

A reframe for “little progress”: stillness as its own intensity, not a failure of output.

Inactivity has a logic of its own, its own language, temporality, architecture, magnificence—even its own magic. It is not a weakness or defect but rather an intensity.

Byung-Chul Han, Vita Contemplativa
3

I am nurturing self-control and productivity. It feels like a constant struggle between the dark and the light forces within me.

Han names the struggle exactly: not dark against light, but a self at war with itself.

No-longer-being-able-to-be-able leads to destructive self-reproach and auto-aggression. The achievement-subject finds itself fighting with itself.

Byung-Chul Han, The Burnout Society
4

Love in every form. Intimate offline communities around every major project I do.

Love not as a single bond but an energy—in every form, around everything made.

love isn’t always a doorway to forever—sometimes it is a door to another love. Always it is an emergent process. Love is an energy of possibility: the possibility of wholeness.

adrienne maree brown, Emergent Strategy
5

A values-aligned “selfhood” that is honest, humble, motivated, curious, unafraid—of failure, loss, rejection. It feels scary, exposing, possibly too late.

The padded suit promises safety. Benjamin says the braver move is to take it off.

We can take off the hot, foam-padded suits we might wear in hopes of safety and belonging. We can transform the hostile environments that try to trap us.

Ruha Benjamin, Imagination: A Manifesto
II Discipline, duty, what is hard
6

My website. Empoweringly vulnerable.

A website is exactly this: you, present and exposed, while absent.

users of correspondence marvel at its power to make the absent (especially the dear absent) present. In Greek antiquity, the term parusia—presence—was used about correspondence.

Jérôme Bourdon, Telepresence: Or, We Have Always Been Ghosts
7

Realigning a lived reality to an ideal. Feels like rebirth.

Not utopia, which is a dream—but the ideal built wide awake. Rebirth as waking.

Whereas utopias are the stuff of dreams and dystopias the stuff of nightmares, ustopias are what we create together when we are wide awake.

Ruha Benjamin, The New Artificial Intelligentsia
8

Trying to nurture patience and perseverance—feels difficult, and a bit like penance.

Perseverance toward people seven generations away. Patience as devotion—close to what “penance” reaches for.

We cannot simply think of our survival; each new generation is responsible to ensure the survival of the seventh generation.

Clarkson, Morrissette & Régallet, Our Responsibility to the Seventh Generation
9

Practice → self-care, nurturing. It feels unnatural, because I had been shut down with duties and responsibilities.

Why self-care feels unnatural: the knowledge of how to live, quietly short-circuited.

These instruments, controlled by marketing, bypass and short-circuit the savoir-vivre of consumers, their knowledge of how to live.

Bernard Stiegler, Automatic Society
10

I am nurturing eros, and it feels transpersonal to tend it.

For Han, Eros is precisely what pulls the self out of circling itself—the transpersonal turn.

The self, thrown back on itself, begins to circle around itself in a worldless movement. Only Eros can defeat anxiety and depression.

Byung-Chul Han, Vita Contemplativa
III Making, creating, becoming
11

Fashion, creativity, Art. Liberating, Motivating.

Creativity as the liberating power to bring into presence what does not yet exist.

Ada Lovelace described the imagination as that which conceives and brings into mental presence that which is far away, or invisible—a faculty she called God-like.

Ada Lovelace, in Imogen Forbes-Macphail, Ada Lovelace’s Poetical Science
12

Nurturing learning and putting myself out there. It feels good to get shit done.

Putting yourself out there as something other than being graded.

what we wanted was to give writing a social role in a classroom, and thus to create a situation in which the writing was read by real readers.

Russ Hunt, What is Inkshedding?
IV Community, the collective
13

I’m nurturing community and building a sense of belonging. It feels hard and overwhelming.

“Students”—meaning no one arrives already fluent. The difficulty is the curriculum.

reminding our species that we belong to this earth and to each other. We need to do the work of honestly engaging in community as students of belonging.

adrienne maree brown, Loving Corrections
14

People endeavoring to bring people together in communities of practice. Feels like god’s work—but should I do my own thing, or nothing?

A community of practice is learning-not-teaching made into a room. An answer to the doubt about whether the gathering matters.

Creative, active individuals can only grow up in a society which emphasizes learning instead of teaching.

Alexander, Ishikawa & Silverstein, A Pattern Language
15

Intention, collective, coordinated, sequential action. Massive. Exciting. Curious how to make it happen.

A founding model of exactly this: intention turned into coordinated collective work.

we are actively committed to struggling against racial, sexual, heterosexual, and class oppression, and see as our particular task the development of integrated analysis and practice.

The Combahee River Collective Statement
V What we leave, where we are going
16

What feeling do we leave behind with people—friend or stranger, or someone who disagrees? One of hope and kindness.

Aloha as the intelligence we meet people with—friend or stranger alike.

Aloha is the intelligence with which we meet life.

Olana Kaipo Ai, in Indigenous Protocol and Artificial Intelligence
17

Self-trust → nurtured in therapy.

Trust given—even to the self—is also trust that grows it.

Trust is a seed that grows with attention and space. The facilitator can be a gardener, or the sun, the water.

adrienne maree brown, Emergent Strategy
18

Navigate retirement life meaningfully. Uncertainty, with too many choices.

Later life as a full, settled stage with its own proper place—not a surplus of loose choices.

Make certain that the full cycle of life is represented and balanced in each community. Set the ideal of a balanced life cycle as a principal guide.

Alexander, Ishikawa & Silverstein, A Pattern Language
19

Emotional safety. Navigating the ocean from a ship with sails.

Safety on the open water reframed as kinship—the wind and the ocean are kin, not threats. You are carried, not exposed.

ways of knowing and speaking that acknowledge kinship networks that extend to animal and plant, wind and rock, mountain and ocean.

Lewis, Arista, Pechawis & Kite, Indigenous Protocol and Artificial Intelligence

Nineteen lines, anonymous, carried in each writer’s own words. Each passage was read in full against its source; quotation marks mark verbatim text, unmarked italics a faithful, attributed compression. The weave holds the room as it was—two responses, one hand, kept company by a wider conversation.
